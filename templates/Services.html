ORAL
 PROPHYLAXIS

Oral Prophylaxis is done for teeth cleaning wherein the dentist uses a scaler, a type of hand instrument. Through this, the tartar, plaque, and those that are hidden between and under the gums are removed from the surfaces of the teeth .

TOOTH
 RESTORATION

Decay, deterioration of a previously placed restoration, or fracture of a tooth leads to missing teeth or parts of the tooth structure becomes missing. To address this problem, tooth restoration is done. Some of the ways of tooth restoration includes fillings, crowns, bridges, implants, and dentures. 

Tooth Extraction 

Tooth Extraction is the process wherein a tooth is removed. There are various reasons as to why this procedure has to be done such as dental cavities, gum disease, dental infections, trauma or injury to the tooth or surrounding bone, wisdom teeth complications, preparation for a dental prosthesis, preparation for dental braces, and baby teeth not falling out at the proper age.

Odontectomy

Odontectomy is the removal of the tooth through separating and opening the gums or removing the bone around the tooth if necessary. This procedure is done when the patient has impacted third molars, embedded supernumerary tooth, misaligned teeth, and tooth/teeth diagnosed with cysts. 

Full and partial dentures

Dentures are used as a removable replacement for missing teeth and surrounding tissues. Full dentures are replacement for all the teeth. On the other hand, partial dentures are used when only some teeth need to be replaced while some are still natural teeth. 

Root canal treatment

Root canal treatment (endodontics) is done to treat infection at the center of a tooth due to bacteria that live in the mouth and invade the tooth. This infection may occur after tooth decay, leaky fillings, and damage to teeth as a result of trauma. Undergoing this dental procedure can save a tooth from removal. 

Fixed Bridge

Bridge fills the space where a tooth or teeth are missing. This restores the bite and maintains the natural shape of the face. Fixed bridge is a type of dental bridge wherein the tooth is molded in reference to the natural tooth. This is cemented making it stable and feel more natural. This artificial tooth is called pontic. Crown is used to secure this pontic.

Jacket crowns

Jacket crown has a shape similar to a tooth used to protect and cover a chipped, broken, or damaged tooth. Through this, there is a permanent restoration of the tooth making it functional and gaining its aesthetic natural look.

Veneers

Veneers cover parts of the teeth. This is ideal for situations when the teeth are in good condition however there is a desire to change its appearance such as shape and color. Its three types include direct composite veneers, indirect composite veneers, and porcelain veneers. Direct composite veneers are composed of composite resin material, which are applied directly to the teeth. Indirect composite veneers have the same material as the direct composite veneers however it varies in application. It can resist more abrasions and fractures compared to direct composite veneers. Porcelain veneers are composed of porcelain.This is cemented to the front of the teeth. These are shaped in such a way that it looks natural.
